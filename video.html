Copenhagen, the capital of Denmark, is a city where historic charm perfectly blends with modern innovation. Known as one of the world’s most livable cities, it is famous for its colorful harbor fronts, royal palaces, and a deep-seated culture of "hygge"—a Danish concept of coziness and contentment. Whether you are wandering through the cobblestone streets of the Old Town or admiring the cutting-edge architecture along the waterfront, the city exudes a relaxed yet sophisticated atmosphere.

The city is a global leader in sustainability and urban planning, most notably seen in its massive cycling culture. In Copenhagen, bicycles often outnumber cars, and an extensive network of cycle paths allows residents and tourists alike to navigate the city safely and quickly. This commitment to a green lifestyle extends to the harbor, which is clean enough to swim in, and the city's ambitious goal to become carbon neutral in the near future.

What interesting things can I do in Copenhagen for 3 days?

  • Day 1: Royal Landmarks and Iconic ViewsStart your journey in the heart of the city at Indre By.
    Witness the Changing of the Guard at Amalienborg Palace before taking a stroll to the iconic Little Mermaid statue.
  • Day 2: Culture, Castles, and Alternative VibesSpend your second day exploring Copenhagen’s rich history and unique social experiments. Visit Rosenborg Castle and its beautiful King’s Garden, which is free to enter and perfect for a picnic lunch
  • Day 3: Art, Adrenaline, and Local NeighborhoodsOn your final day, choose between art and thrills. You can visit the Ny Carlsberg Glyptotek (which often has discounted or free entry days) or head to Tivoli Gardens, one of the world's oldest amusement parks.

For food lovers and culture seekers, Copenhagen offers an incredible variety of experiences. It is a world-renowned culinary destination, home to many Michelin-starred restaurants and a vibrant street food scene. Beyond the plate, you can explore the magical Tivoli Gardens, one of the oldest amusement parks in the world, or visit the alternative community of Freetown Christiania. The city's museums, such as the National Museum and the Glyptoteket, provide deep dives into everything from Viking history to classical art.
